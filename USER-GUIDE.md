## ðŸš€ Getting Started

### What is an Escrow Service?

An escrow service acts as a trusted third party that holds funds during a transaction between a buyer and seller. The funds are only released to the seller when the buyer confirms they're satisfied with the goods or services received.

###  Benefits:

- **Security**: Your money is protected until you receive what you paid for
- **Trust**: Both parties are protected from fraud
- **Transparency**: Track every step of your transaction
- **Dispute Resolution**: Professional mediation if issues arise

### Starting a Transaction

#### Step 1: Create Transaction

1. **Navigate to Dashboard**
   - Click "Create Transaction" button

   - Click "Create Transaction"

#### Step 2: Fund Escrow

Once seller accepts:

1. **Click "Fund Escrow"**

   - Service fee: 2.5% of transaction amount

2. **Choose Payment Method**
   - Paystack (Card payment)
   - Bank transfer
   - Wallet balance

3. **Complete Payment**
   - Follow payment gateway instructions
   - Enter card details if paying by card
   - Confirm payment

4. **Confirmation**
   - You'll see "Payment Successful" message
   - Seller is notified to begin work
   - Funds are held securely in escrow

#### Step 3: Monitor Progress

1. **Track Status**
   - View transaction status on dashboard
   - Check milestone completion
   - Communicate with seller via chat

2. **Request Updates**
   - Message seller for progress updates
   - Review delivered work
   - Request revisions if needed

#### Step 4: Release Payment

When satisfied with work:

1. **Review Deliverables**
   - Check all requirements met
   - Test/inspect the product/service
   - Request changes if needed

2. **Approve Transaction**
   - Click "Approve & Release Payment"
   - Confirm your decision

## âš–ï¸ Disputes

### When to Raise a Dispute

Raise a dispute if:
- Seller didn't deliver as agreed
- Work quality doesn't meet requirements
- Seller is unresponsive
- Buyer refuses legitimate payment
- Terms were violated

### How to Raise a Dispute

1. **Open Transaction**
   - Go to transaction details
   - Click "Raise Dispute"

2. **Provide Details**
   - Explain the issue clearly
   - Upload evidence (screenshots, files)
   - Suggest resolution

3. **Submit**
   - Dispute is logged
   - Other party is notified
   - Admin reviews case

### Dispute Resolution Process

1. **Initial Review** (1-2 days)
   - Admin reviews details
   - Both parties provide evidence
   - Timeline established

2. **Investigation** (3-5 days)
   - Evidence examined
   - Both parties heard
   - Additional info requested if needed

3. **Resolution** (1-2 days)
   - Decision made
   - Funds distributed accordingly
   - Both parties notified

### Possible Outcomes

- **Full refund to buyer**: If seller failed to deliver
- **Partial refund**: If work partially completed
- **Full payment to seller**: If buyer claim invalid
- **Split decision**: Based on percentage completed

### Best Practices

**To Avoid Disputes:**
- Communicate clearly from start
- Document everything
- Set realistic expectations
- Keep all conversations on platform
- Meet agreed deadlines

**During Disputes:**
- Stay professional
- Provide all evidence
- Respond promptly
- Accept fair resolutions
